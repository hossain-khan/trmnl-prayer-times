The five daily obligatory prayers (Salah) in Islam, ordered from dawn to night, are Fajr (dawn), Dhuhr (midday), Asr (afternoon), Maghrib (sunset), and Isha (night). These prayers are foundational, with specific Arabic names and times: 

- Fajr (فجر): Dawn, before sunrise.
- Dhuhr (ظهر): Midday, after the sun passes its highest.
- Asr (عصر): Late afternoon.
- Maghrib (مغرب): Dusk, just after sunset.
- Isha (عشاء): Night, between sunset and midnight.

Arabic as fajr (فجر), dhuhr (ظهر), asr (عصر), maghrib (مغرب), and isha (عشاء).
